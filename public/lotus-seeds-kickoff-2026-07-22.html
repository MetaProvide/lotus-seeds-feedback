What Lotus is

One home for a Dharma centre, one account for every practitioner

Lotus brings events, registrations, lodging, meals, payments, and fiscal compliance into a single platform, built for small teams and limited budgets. A practitioner gets one account that travels with them to every centre they attend.

1 Centre

The tools your centre runs on every day: events, registration, lodging, meals, payments, compliance.

2 Network

Cross-centre discovery. Visitors find events, teachers, and programmes across the network, no account needed.

3 Network of networks

An organisation tier above centres, turning Lotus into a platform for global communities of practice.

Our north star

We measure success by usability and happiness of use

Not feature count. Not screens shipped. The question we keep asking is simple: does using Lotus feel calm, clear, and genuinely helpful to the people running a centre and the people attending it?

What we optimise for

Fewer steps, less confusion, less admin overhead. Software that gets out of the way so centres can focus on practice, not tooling.

Why it matters

Centres run on small teams and goodwill. If the tool is a burden, it fails, no matter how many features it has. Ease of use is the product.

Why the Seeds matter

You are not testers. You are shaping the product.

Lotus only becomes calm and usable if real operators pressure-test it before launch. Each build cycle, the Seeds try the new work with real centre scenarios and tell us what feels wrong. That signal is what makes the September go-live safe.

  • You catch what we can't see. We build it; you live it. The friction you feel is the friction every future centre would feel.
  • You set priorities. Your feedback feeds directly into what we fix and build next, not a suggestion box that goes nowhere.
  • You de-risk the launch. Problems found now are cheap. Problems found after go-live are expensive and public.

Your role & commitment

What being a Seed actually involves

Every ~3 weeks

A ~1h call with us where we walk through the cycle's new work together. Separately, expect ~30 min on your own trying it out before we meet.

Try it with real scenarios

Use the new features the way your centre actually would, not a scripted demo. Register a real event, add a real programme, see where it breaks.

Tell us what feels wrong

Log anything: a confusing step, a missing option, a bug. Small friction counts. That is exactly the signal we need.

Who should join

The person who runs registrations or operations day to day. Board or governance questions have their own separate track.

The bigger picture

Where Lotus is headed, and what we're committing to now

Lotus is built in four gates. Two are locked in for this launch. The other two are real and on the map, but still taking shape, so we want to be honest about what we can promise you today.

Gate ACommitting now

Events & Registration

Event catalogue, sessions, registration and waitlist. The core operators touch every day.

Gate BCommitting now

Payments & Finance

Invoicing, Stripe payments, refunds, tax and fiscal compliance. Money handled correctly.

Gate CStill shaping

Lodging & Meals

Rooms, beds and kitchen for residential centres. The hardest domain, scope and timing still open.

Gate DStill shaping

Community & Memberships

Memberships, class passes, volunteers and comms. On the roadmap, not yet dated.

Now: Gate A + B, targeting the September launch Later: Gate C + D, we'll shape these together with you
